category: science

I used to think a hangover was just "dehydration plus regret," but the more I dug into the science, the more I realized your B vitamins are quietly caught in the crossfire every time you drink. Here's what's actually going on, and why it matters the next morning.

## Alcohol and Thiamine Don't Mix

Vitamin B1, also known as thiamine, is one of the first casualties of heavy drinking. Heavy alcohol use can inflame your digestive tract and block the absorption of vitamins, and it's specifically tied to thiamine deficiency ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)). That's a big deal because thiamine is involved in how your body converts food into usable energy. When your gut is inflamed and struggling to absorb it, you're not getting the B1 you need even if your diet looks fine on paper.

This isn't just an occasional-drinker footnote either. It's one of the reasons "just eat before you go out" only gets you so far. If alcohol is actively interfering with absorption in your gut, the vitamins in your food have a harder time getting where they need to go in the first place ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)).

## It's Not Just About Dehydration

Most of us grew up thinking a hangover is basically your body crying out for water. But the research points somewhere else. Hangover symptoms are linked to an inflammatory response, and there isn't good evidence that dehydration is actually the primary driver ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). Alcohol hangover is driven by multiple mechanisms working together, including that inflammatory response, rather than any single cause ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6761819/)).

Why does that matter for B vitamins specifically? Because it means the rough-morning-after feeling isn't a simple math problem you can solve by chugging a glass of water. Your body is dealing with several overlapping stressors at once, on top of whatever nutrient gaps the alcohol left behind, which is exactly why a single-angle fix rarely does the job.

## Where B Vitamins Fit Into Recovery

None of this means B vitamins are some magic cure, and I want to be careful here because they're not. There's ongoing research into other compounds too, like glutathione, which has been studied for its role in alcohol metabolism and hangover symptoms in a randomized, placebo-controlled trial ([Nutrients](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262)). The science on hangovers is still evolving, and anyone who tells you they've "solved" it with one ingredient is oversimplifying.

What I can say is that keeping your B vitamin intake in mind, especially around heavier drinking, is a reasonable piece of the puzzle. It's about giving your body a more direct way to get support when your usual channels are already under strain.

At the end of the day, your body is dealing with a lot after a night of drinking, from inflammation to absorption issues to whatever nutrients got depleted along the way. Understanding that gives you a much better starting point than just blaming it all on dehydration and reaching for another glass of water.
