I've done the rehearsal dinner, the ceremony, the reception, and the recovery brunch all in one 48 hour stretch more times than I can count, and I can tell you it's a different beast than a regular Saturday night out. A wedding weekend is a multi-day event with its own pacing, and if you don't plan for it, you end up wrecked by the Sunday goodbye brunch instead of actually enjoying it.

## Pace Yourself Like It's a Marathon, Not a Sprint

The biggest mistake I see people make is treating Friday night like it's the whole party. You've got the welcome drinks, then the rehearsal dinner with an open bar, then the actual wedding day with champagne toasts and a reception that goes until 1am, then usually a farewell brunch the next morning. That's a lot of alcohol spread across a short window, and from my own experience, front-loading all of it into Friday night just guarantees you're running on empty by Saturday's ceremony.

My honest advice is to know your own limits and stretch your drinks out over the whole weekend instead of blowing your budget on night one. Treat it like pacing yourself for three days, not one.

## What's Actually Happening Inside Your Body

A lot of people assume a hangover is mostly about being dehydrated, but that's not really the full picture. Hangover symptoms are more closely tied to your body's inflammatory response than to dehydration itself, and there isn't strong evidence that dehydration is the main driver ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). Research on the topic backs this up too. It's generally understood that a hangover comes from a mix of different mechanisms working together, including that inflammatory response, rather than any one single cause ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6761819/)).

That matters for a wedding weekend because it means chugging water alone between events isn't going to be your silver bullet. Your body is dealing with more than just fluid loss, so it helps to think about recovery as a multi-part job, not a one-step fix.

## Nutrients Take a Hit Too

Something people don't talk about enough is what a weekend of heavier drinking does to your vitamin levels. Heavy alcohol use can irritate your digestive tract and get in the way of vitamin absorption, and it's linked to thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency specifically ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)). Over a single wild Friday that's probably not a huge deal, but over a three day wedding stretch with multiple nights of drinking back to back, that's the kind of thing that can leave you feeling foggier than usual by the time you're hugging the happy couple goodbye.

This is where I bring up Pulsar, because it's genuinely part of my own wedding weekend kit at this point. I put a patch on before the rehearsal dinner and another before the reception, since a transdermal patch delivers its ingredients through your skin and skips the first-pass metabolism that a pill has to go through, which can mean more of what's in it actually gets where it's going ([Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/delivery-methods-the-patch-versus-the-oral-route)). It's not a magic fix and I'm not going to pretend it erases a weekend of champagne toasts, but it's designed to support your body through the parts of a long event that wear you down, and I like that I don't have to remember to swallow anything at 11pm on the dance floor.

## Building Your Own Recovery Kit

If you're planning to survive an entire wedding weekend and still be a decent human at brunch, build yourself a small kit before you leave home. Some researchers have looked specifically at antioxidants like glutathione and how they interact with alcohol metabolism and hangover symptoms in controlled trials ([Nutrients](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262)), so it's worth paying attention to what you're putting in your body across the weekend, not just during the reception itself. Pack electrolytes, a few good snacks, comfortable shoes for the after party, and Pulsar patches for each big night. Give yourself permission to sit one round out here and there. Nobody at that wedding is going to remember whether you had four drinks or six, but you'll remember whether you made it through the whole weekend feeling like yourself.
