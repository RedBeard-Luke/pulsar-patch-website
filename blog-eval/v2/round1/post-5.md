---
title: My Pre-Night-Out Routine That Saves My Morning
category: lifestyle
---

I used to think the only thing that mattered on a night out was what happened between the first drink and the last one. These days I spend way more energy on the twenty minutes before I even leave my apartment, because that's the window that actually decides how I feel the next day. Here's the routine I've built, piece by piece, and why each part earns its spot.

## Eating Something Real Before I Go Out

I never leave the house on an empty stomach anymore. It doesn't have to be a full meal, but I want protein and some carbs in me before the first drink touches my lips. Part of why I'm strict about this is that heavy drinking can inflame your digestive tract and get in the way of how your body absorbs vitamins, which is part of why alcohol use is linked to thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency in the first place ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)). If my gut is already dealing with food and doing its normal job, I feel like I'm not starting the night at a deficit.

## Managing Expectations Around Dehydration

For years I assumed a hangover was basically just dehydration, so I'd chug a liter of water before bed and call it a plan. Turns out that story is incomplete. The current thinking from Cedars-Sinai is that hangover symptoms are more closely tied to an inflammatory response in the body than to dehydration itself, and there isn't good evidence that dehydration is the primary driver ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). Research reviewers have made a similar point, describing the alcohol hangover as something driven by multiple mechanisms and mediators, including inflammation, rather than any single cause ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6761819/)). So yes, I still drink water. But I stopped pretending it's the whole answer, and that changed what else I bother to prep before I walk out the door.

## What I Actually Put in My Body Before I Leave

This is the part of the routine that's evolved the most. I'm not chasing one magic fix, because based on what I just described, there probably isn't one. Instead I think about it as covering a few different bases at once. Glutathione is one ingredient that's actually been studied for its role in alcohol metabolism and hangover outcomes, including in a randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial ([Nutrients/MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262)), which is part of why I look for it in whatever I'm using that night. This is also where Pulsar comes into my routine. I put a Pulsar patch on about 30 to 45 minutes before I plan to have my first drink, so it's already working by the time I'm ordering. I like that it's a patch and not another pill I have to remember to swallow, because transdermal delivery gets actives through the skin and sidesteps the first-pass metabolism that oral pills go through, which can improve how much of the active actually reaches your bloodstream ([Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/delivery-methods-the-patch-versus-the-oral-route)). I'm not expecting it to erase a night out. I think of it as giving my body some backup for the parts of the night I can't fully control.

## The Last Few Minutes Before I Walk Out

Right before I leave, I do one more pass: patch on, water bottle filled, food already in my stomach, and I decide roughly how many drinks I'm actually comfortable with that night instead of figuring it out drink by drink. None of this is about controlling every variable, because a night out has plenty of those and I can't plan around all of them. It's about walking out the door having already done the handful of things that are actually in my control, so that whatever happens after, I gave my morning-self a fighting chance. My Pulsar patch is one piece of that, not the whole plan, and that's exactly how I want it. Supporting my body through the rough patches is a much more honest goal than promising myself a perfect morning, and it's the one that's actually kept this routine working for me.
