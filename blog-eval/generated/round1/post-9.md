---
title: The Zero-Proof Spritz I Make On My Nights Off
category: recipes
---

I take a night off the booze most weeks now, and the hardest part was never the drinking itself, it was standing at a party with a warm can of soda while everyone else has something that fizzes and looks like an occasion. So I built a zero-proof spritz that actually feels like a drink, not a consolation prize, and I want to walk you through how I make it.

## Why I Started Skipping A Round

The push to cut back came from noticing how rough the mornings after a heavier night could get. Hangover severity tracks pretty closely with how much you actually drink, so cutting the volume in half on a given night, or skipping it entirely, is one of the simplest levers there is [Nature (Scientific Reports)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-92676-0). It's also just easier on sleep. A lot of what makes a hangover miserable comes down to dehydration, inflammation, and disrupted sleep, and all three get worse the more you drink late into the night [Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers). A zero-proof night doesn't touch any of that, so I wake up actually rested, and that alone keeps me coming back to this drink.

## The Spritz Itself

Here's my go-to build, made for one big glass over ice:

- 3 oz sparkling water, the more aggressive the bubbles the better
- 2 oz unsweetened tart cherry juice
- 1 oz fresh grapefruit juice
- A few thin slices of fresh ginger, muddled lightly in the glass before you add anything else
- A rosemary sprig and a twist of orange peel to finish

I muddle the ginger first because it wakes up the whole glass, gives it a little heat that plays against the sparkling water. Ginger has also been studied for supporting digestion and easing nausea, so it's a genuinely useful thing to have in a drink you're sipping slowly over a few hours [Nutrients (MDPI)](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262). The tart cherry juice gives it the deep red color that makes it look like something with a kick, and the grapefruit brings the bitter edge that a real spritz needs. Build it in a wine glass, big ice cubes, and nobody at the party is going to ask you why you're not drinking.

## Making It A Ritual, Not A Compromise

The thing that took the longest to figure out wasn't the recipe, it was the mindset. If I treat the zero-proof spritz like a lesser version of a real drink, I resent it by 9pm. If I treat it like its own thing, with its own glass, its own garnish, its own little ceremony of muddling the ginger and building it slow, it stops feeling like I'm missing out. I've started doing this two or three nights a week regardless of whether I'm around other people, just because I like having something in hand that isn't a beer.

This is also where Pulsar fits into my week, honestly. On the nights I do drink, the patch is part of my routine before I go out, and on the nights I don't, the spritz is what replaces the ritual of pouring something. Neither one is trying to be medicine, they're both just tools for feeling like myself the next day.

## A Few Swaps Worth Trying

If you don't have tart cherry juice on hand, pomegranate juice works almost as well and leans a little sweeter. Swap the grapefruit for blood orange in the fall, and swap the rosemary for a few mint leaves if you want something lighter and more summery. The ginger stays no matter what, it's the backbone of the whole drink. Play with the ratios too, more sparkling water if you want something you can sip all night, less if you want it punchier and more like a real cocktail in a smaller glass.

I don't think of this as a "sober drink," I think of it as just a good drink that happens to have nothing in it. That distinction matters more than it sounds like it should.
