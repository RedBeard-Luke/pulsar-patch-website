---
title: The Pre-Night-Out Routine That Saves Your Morning
category: lifestyle
---

I used to think the only prep that mattered for a big night out was picking an outfit and figuring out the group chat logistics. Turns out the ten minutes before you walk out the door matter just as much as anything you do at 1am. Over the last couple years I've built a little pre-game ritual that has nothing to do with drinking less and everything to do with setting my body up so the next morning doesn't wreck me. Here's what that routine actually looks like.

## Start With Water, Not a Shot

The first thing I do before I even get dressed is drink a big glass of water. It sounds boring, but hangovers are largely driven by dehydration, inflammation, and disrupted sleep, and symptoms tend to peak as your blood alcohol returns to zero the next morning ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). If I'm already behind on water before the first drink even happens, I'm digging a hole for my future self. So I treat pre-hydration like part of getting ready, right up there with brushing my teeth.

I also keep in mind that how rough tomorrow feels isn't totally random. Hangover severity varies from person to person and tracks with how much alcohol you actually drink ([Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-92676-0)), so part of my "routine" is honestly just being a little more intentional about pacing once I'm out, not just what I do beforehand.

## Patch Up Before You Head Out

This is the part of my routine that changed the game for me. I put on my Pulsar patch about 30-45 minutes before I leave the house, so it's already working by the time I'm ordering my first drink. I like that it's a patch and not another pill I have to remember to swallow with food, because transdermal delivery gets actives through the skin and skips the first-pass gastrointestinal and liver breakdown that oral pills go through ([Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/delivery-methods-the-patch-versus-the-oral-route)). For me that means less "did I even absorb that" anxiety and one less thing to think about mid-party.

Pulsar isn't a magic eraser and I'm not going to pretend it is. It's designed to support your body through the rough parts of the morning after, not promise you'll wake up like nothing happened. But having it already on and working before the night starts, instead of scrambling for something at 2am, is the whole point.

## Feed Your Body What Alcohol Takes Away

The other piece of my pre-night-out routine is eating something real, not just bar snacks. Alcohol depletes thiamine, also known as vitamin B1, and can get in the way of your body absorbing and using what you do have ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)), so I try to eat a meal with some actual nutritional weight before I head out instead of drinking on an empty stomach. It's a small thing but it's one of the few parts of this whole routine that's completely in my control.

I've also started keeping ginger chews in my bag, mostly out of habit at this point. Ginger has been studied for supporting digestion and easing nausea ([Nutrients, MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262)), so if my stomach feels off later in the night, it's an easy thing to reach for. None of this is a cure for anything, it's just stacking small, supportive habits so the morning after doesn't feel like a total loss.
