category: science

I used to think a hangover was just "dehydration plus regret," but the more I dig into the actual science, the more I realize your B vitamins are quietly taking a beating every time you drink. Let's talk about what's really going on under the hood.

## The B1 Problem Nobody Talks About

Thiamine, also known as vitamin B1, is one of the first casualties of a night of drinking. Alcohol consumption depletes thiamine and can impair how well your body absorbs and uses it in the first place ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)). That's a big deal because thiamine is involved in how your cells turn food into usable energy. When it's running low, you're not just tired, you're operating with less fuel in the tank than usual.

This is part of why the day after can feel so much worse than "I just didn't sleep enough." Your body is dealing with a nutrient shortfall on top of everything else.

## Why Your Whole Body Feels the Hit

It's not only the vitamin depletion doing the damage. Hangovers in general are driven by a mix of dehydration, inflammation, and disrupted sleep, and symptoms tend to peak right around when your blood alcohol returns to zero ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). So you've got the B vitamin drain happening at the exact same time your body is fighting inflammation and trying to rehydrate. It's a pile-on, not a single issue, which is why "just drink water" never feels like enough.

Also worth knowing: how rough you feel the next day isn't random. Hangover severity varies from person to person and is tied to how much alcohol you actually consumed ([Nature, Scientific Reports](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-92676-0)). So if last night hit different than the night before, your body isn't being dramatic, there's real variability at play.

## What I'd Actually Take Away From This

If you take one thing from all this, let it be that your next-day rough patch isn't just "in your head" or purely about water intake. There's a real nutritional component happening alongside the dehydration and inflammation, and it's worth taking seriously instead of just chugging coffee and hoping for the best. I'm not saying panic every time you have a drink, I'm saying it helps to know what your body is actually dealing with so you can be a little kinder to it afterward.
