---
title: How To Actually Enjoy Brunch The Morning After
category: lifestyle
---

I used to treat the morning after a big night out like something to survive, not something to enjoy. Brunch was just a plate I stared at while I waited to feel human again. Somewhere along the way I decided that was backwards, and now brunch is my favorite part of the whole night out, hangover and all.

## Pick A Table, Not A Drive-Thru

The instinct after a rough night is to grab the fastest, greasiest thing within walking distance and eat it standing over the sink. I get it, but slowing down actually helps more than speeding up. Sitting at an actual table with your friends, ordering coffee first, and giving yourself twenty minutes before the food even arrives changes the whole morning. It's less about the food and more about giving your body a minute to catch up. A lot of what you're feeling that morning comes down to dehydration, inflammation, and sleep that got cut short, and your symptoms are usually worst right around when your blood alcohol finally hits zero ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). So the slow start isn't laziness, it's just working with your body's timeline instead of against it.

## Build A Plate That Actually Helps

I used to order whatever sounded good at 1am and regret it at 11am. Now I think about what's actually going on my plate. Ginger shows up in a lot of my brunch orders these days, whether it's a ginger-lemon tea or a ginger scone, because it's been studied for supporting digestion and easing nausea ([Nutrients](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262)). I also try not to skip the eggs or whole grains, since drinking alcohol depletes thiamine, also known as vitamin B1, and can make it harder for your body to absorb and use what's left ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)). None of this is a magic fix, it's just building a plate that works with you instead of against you.

## Know Your Own Hangover

Something I had to learn the hard way is that comparing my hangover to my friend's is a losing game. How rough you feel the next day varies a lot from person to person and tends to track with how much you actually drank the night before ([Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-92676-0)). So if your buddy is bouncing around ordering a second mimosa while you're squinting at the menu, that's not a character flaw, that's just biology. I've stopped trying to keep up with anyone else's morning and started just paying attention to what my own body needs that day, whether that's a quiet booth in the corner or extra water before the coffee even hits the table.

## Where Pulsar Fits In

This is where Pulsar comes into the picture for me. I put the patch on before I even leave the bar the night before, so by the time I'm sitting down for brunch I've already given my body a head start instead of scrambling the next morning. Part of why I like the patch format is that it delivers through the skin, which skips the first pass through your gut and liver that oral pills have to go through ([Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/delivery-methods-the-patch-versus-the-oral-route)). Pulsar also leans on ingredients like NAC, which is a precursor to glutathione, one of the antioxidants your body relies on during its normal detox processes ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6761819/)). I'm not telling you the patch turns brunch into a miracle, it's just one more thing working in the background so I can actually taste my food instead of white-knuckling through it.

## Make It A Ritual, Not A Recovery Mission

The biggest shift for me was mental. Brunch isn't the finish line of a bad night, it's part of the fun, the same way the night out was. Order the thing you actually want, not just the thing that sounds "safe." Sit outside if the light doesn't hurt too much. Let the conversation replay the best parts of the night before instead of everyone comparing who feels worse. That reframe alone did more for my Sunday mornings than any single food or drink ever did.
