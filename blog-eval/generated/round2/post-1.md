I get asked this a lot: why a patch, why not just take a pill or mix a powder into water? The short answer is that your skin is actually a pretty clever doorway into your bloodstream, and it skips a bunch of steps your gut has to go through first. Once I understood how that worked, the patch format made a lot more sense to me, so let's walk through it.

## What Your Gut Does To A Pill Before It Ever Reaches You

When you swallow a capsule, it has a long trip ahead of it. It has to survive your stomach acid, get absorbed through your intestinal wall, then pass through your liver before it ever reaches the rest of your body. That last stop is called first-pass metabolism, and it's the reason oral supplements lose a chunk of their potency before they do anything useful. Transdermal delivery, on the other hand, sends ingredients straight through the skin and into the bloodstream, bypassing that gut-and-liver gauntlet entirely ([Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/delivery-methods-the-patch-versus-the-oral-route)). That's the whole reason patches exist as a delivery method in the first place, not just for us but for things like nicotine and motion sickness patches too.

I think about it like a shortcut through the back door instead of waiting in line at the front. Same building, way less friction to get in.

## Why That Matters The Morning After A Night Out

Here's where it gets personal for Pulsar. A rough night can leave you dealing with dehydration, inflammation, and disrupted sleep, and a lot of people feel worst right around the time their blood alcohol level drops back to zero ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). That's exactly the window when your stomach is the least reliable partner you could ask for. If you've ever tried to choke down a pill or a big glass of water with electrolyte powder while your stomach is doing somersaults, you know what I mean. Swallowing anything can feel like a chore, and there's a real chance some of what you take won't stay down long enough to matter.

A patch sidesteps that whole problem. You stick it on, and it doesn't ask your stomach for permission.

## Picking Ingredients That Actually Make Sense For Skin Delivery

Not every ingredient is a good fit for this method, so we built Pulsar around ones that make sense both for what your body might need after drinking and for how they move through skin. Take thiamine, or vitamin B1. Alcohol can deplete your body's thiamine stores and interfere with how well you absorb and use it in the first place ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)), which is part of why we include it in the formula alongside the rest of the blend. Getting it in through the skin instead of relying on a stressed-out gut felt like the more sensible route, especially during the exact hours when your digestive system is least cooperative.

None of this means Pulsar is some miracle fix. It's a supplement designed to support your body through a tough morning, not a cure for anything. But the delivery method itself, going through skin instead of your gut, is a genuinely different way of getting ingredients on board, and it's the reason we built the brand around a patch instead of another pill or powder.

## The Bigger Picture

At the end of the day, a patch is just a different route to the same destination: your bloodstream. It happens to be a route that doesn't depend on your stomach cooperating, doesn't get filtered hard by your liver before it circulates, and stays on you for hours instead of asking you to remember a second or third dose. For a product meant to help you feel more like yourself after a big night, that combination of convenience and a more direct path just made sense to us.
