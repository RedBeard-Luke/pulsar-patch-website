title: The Zero-Proof Spritz I Make on My Nights Off
category: recipes
---

I take at least two nights off from drinking every week, and for a long time those nights felt like a punishment. Everyone else had a glass of something bubbly and pretty, and I had tap water in a coffee mug. So I built myself a spritz that has zero alcohol in it but still feels like an occasion, and it has become one of my favorite things to make, booze or no booze.

## Why I Bother With a Night Off At All

A night off isn't about guilt, it's about giving my body a break. Hangovers are driven by dehydration, inflammation, and disrupted sleep, and the rough feeling the next morning tends to peak as your blood alcohol works its way back down to zero ([Cedars-Sinai](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/stories-and-insights/healthy-living/science-of-hangovers)). I also notice that how bad I feel the day after a big night tracks pretty closely with how much I actually drank, and that pattern shows up in research too, since hangover severity varies from person to person and is tied to the amount of alcohol consumed ([Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-92676-0)). None of that means one drink is doom and a night off is salvation, it just means giving my system a lighter week is a reasonable thing to build into my routine. Alcohol also drains your body's stores of thiamine, or vitamin B1, and can make it harder for your body to absorb and use what thiamine you do have ([Alcohol and Drug Foundation](https://adf.org.au/insights/alcohol-related-thiamine-deficiency/)), which is one more reason a break night doesn't feel like a wasted night to me.

## Building the Spritz

Here's the actual recipe. I start with a base of half sparkling water and half a tart, unsweetened juice like pomegranate or grapefruit, poured over a full glass of ice so it stays cold and doesn't go flat too fast. Then I add a splash of fresh lime, a few dashes of a good non-alcoholic bitters, and a small piece of fresh ginger muddled right in the glass. I lean on ginger because I genuinely like the bite it gives the drink, and it's also been studied for its anti-nausea and digestive-support properties, which is a nice bonus on a night when my stomach has had a big meal to deal with ([Nutrients, MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/16/19/3262)). I finish it with a sprig of mint or a twist of orange peel, mostly because it makes the whole thing look like I put effort into it, which I did.

## Making It Feel Like a Real Drink

The trick to a zero-proof night not feeling like a downgrade is presentation. I always use my nicest wine glass, never a plain tumbler, and I never skip the garnish. I also treat it like a real cocktail hour, meaning I sit down, I'm not rushing, and I'm not just gulping it standing at the counter. Little rituals like that are honestly most of what makes a drink feel special in the first place, and they cost nothing extra.

## Where Pulsar Fits Into My Week

I built Pulsar for the nights when I do decide to drink, not the nights off, but the two go together in my head now. Pulsar is a transdermal patch, meaning it delivers its ingredients through your skin instead of through a pill you swallow, which lets it skip the first pass through your gut and liver that oral supplements have to go through ([Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/delivery-methods-the-patch-versus-the-oral-route)). On the nights I do have a few drinks, I put on a patch beforehand because it's designed to support my body through the parts of the next morning that tend to be rough. On my zero-proof nights, I don't need it at all, and that contrast is part of why I like having both options in my back pocket. A night off isn't a rule I'm enforcing on myself, it's just one more tool, right alongside the patch, for feeling like myself more often.

## The Recipe, Written Out

For anyone who wants to just copy the thing: fill a wine glass with ice, add 3 ounces of sparkling water, 3 ounces of unsweetened pomegranate or grapefruit juice, a squeeze of half a lime, three dashes of non-alcoholic bitters, and a small piece of muddled fresh ginger. Stir gently, don't shake, and garnish with mint or an orange twist. It takes about ninety seconds to make and it's become my go-to on any night I'm sitting one out.
